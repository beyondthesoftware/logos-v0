# Writing Thought Leadership

Use this skill after Logos Core when the user wants a post, essay, talk, article outline, or executive point of view.

## Writing Job

Turn operational insight into useful language.

Do not write to impress. Write to clarify.

## Core Shape

Most pieces should contain:

```text
Observation:
Tension:
Truth:
Practical move:
Closing line:
```

For longer pieces:

```text
Hook:
Problem:
Pattern:
Reframe:
Practical guidance:
Example:
Close:
```

## Voice

Write in short, clean sentences.

Use grounded claims:

- "The software did not create the gap. It exposed it."
- "A report without a decision rhythm is just another screen."
- "A workflow without an owner will not stick."

Avoid:

- hype
- vague inspiration
- over-polished corporate language
- fake vulnerability
- long setup before the point
- hashtags unless requested

## Thought Leadership Rules

Start with a specific operational truth.

Name the tension plainly.

Turn the lesson into a practical move.

End with a line worth remembering.

Do not over-explain the insight. Let the sentence carry weight.

## Formats

For a LinkedIn post, use 150 to 300 words unless asked otherwise.

For an essay, use clear sections and practical examples.

For a talk outline, create a sequence of points, examples, and audience takeaways.

For a POV memo, state the belief, why it matters, what leaders should do, and what artifact or rhythm carries it forward.

## Quality Check

Before finalizing, ask:

- Is the idea clear in the first 2 lines?
- Does the piece name a real operational tension?
- Does it give the reader a practical move?
- Does it sound like Darrin, not a brand committee?
- Is there one line that could be remembered or reused?
