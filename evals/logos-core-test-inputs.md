# Logos Core Test Inputs

Use these prompts to test whether Logos Core produces useful responses.

## Test 1: Software Complaint

Our ERP implementation is behind. The team says the system is too rigid, consultants say the process keeps changing, and leadership keeps asking for status reports.

Expected traits:

- identifies the visible issue as ERP delay
- identifies the real issue as process ownership and decision rights
- recommends a decision rhythm
- creates an artifact such as decision log or process ownership map

## Test 2: Metrics Without Behavior Change

We started tracking on-time delivery every day. The number is still bad. People look at the report but nothing changes.

Expected traits:

- names that the metric lacks follow-up habit
- recommends owner and operating review
- focuses on exceptions and decisions
- avoids just recommending more reporting

## Test 3: Leadership Alignment

The executive team agrees in the meeting, but afterward each department does something different.

Expected traits:

- identifies false alignment
- clarifies decisions and tradeoffs
- recommends one shared artifact
- recommends a review rhythm

## Test 4: Too Much Data

We have reports everywhere. Sales, finance, operations, and service all have different versions of the truth.

Expected traits:

- separates data volume from data usefulness
- identifies ownership and definitions
- recommends metric definitions and decision cadence
- avoids building another dashboard as first move

## Test 5: Follow-Through

People leave meetings motivated, but two weeks later nobody remembers what they were supposed to do.

Expected traits:

- identifies missing commitment capture
- assigns meeting owner
- recommends recurring review
- produces commitment tracker or meeting close format

## Scoring Rubric

Score each response from 0 to 2 on each dimension:

- Signal: separates what matters from noise
- Real issue: identifies root issue underneath the visible problem
- Pattern: names the recurring behavior or blind spot
- Decision: clarifies what decision must be made
- Action: gives concrete next moves
- Owner: assigns or recommends ownership
- Rhythm: creates follow-up cadence
- Artifact: produces or recommends reusable output
- Style: direct, practical, plain-English
- Restraint: avoids overbuilding

Total possible score: 20.

A strong response scores 16 or higher.
